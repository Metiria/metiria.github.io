Angelo Li Rosi

Keys

Lentini, Italy

Music Education

Angelo Li Rosi (born in Lentini on May 8, 2002) has been playing the piano since the age of fifteen. After completing his studies at a scientific high school, he enrolled at the P.I. Tchaikovsky State Conservatory in Nocera Terinese, where he is currently pursuing a master degree in Jazz Piano.

For several years, he has been an active member of the Piazza Scienza e Lavoro’s musical laboratory in Lentini, under the esteemed direction of Carlo Cattano and Antonio Moncada.

Bands and Projects

His recording experience includes the single Verde Luna, recorded at Roberto Cavalli’s studio.

Currently, Li Rosi performs with various ensembles for live events in clubs and public venues. He also serves as the keyboardist for the band Metiria, where he contributes to the group’s evolving musical direction.

Inspirations

His artistic vision is shaped by a deep appreciation for jazz masters and contemporary innovators. His primary influences and essential recordings include: Bud Powell (The Amazing Bud Powell, Vol. 1), Aaron Parks (Invisible Cinema), Thelonious Monk (Underground), Charles Mingus (Mingus Ah Um), Emmet Cohen, Kenny Garrett (Songbook).

Style

Angelo Li Rosi possesses a sophisticated harmonic command and the ability to improvise across diverse genres. He is adept at composing arrangements for ensembles, seamlessly integrating elements of jazz and funk into his original works. His creative process is marked by a meticulous — at times exhaustive — focus on structural integrity and the intricate interplay between instruments.

Other Relevant Information

Outside of his musical commitments, he enjoys spending time outdoors, with personal interests including fishing, playing basketball, and retreating to the countryside.
