Arturo Caflisch

Guitar

Catania, Italy

Music Education

Arturo Caflisch (born in Catania, August 2, 2002) is an Italian-Swiss (or better Sicilian-Grison) guitarist.

After beginning to noodle on his mother’s 12 string guitar, Arturo started taking acoustic guitar group lessons at age of 16 and switched to electric soon after. Fabio Inglese pushed him into getting private lessons and taught him the basics of technique and touch.

After some years alternating private lessons with periods of self taught learning, twenty-one years old Arturo focused his attention on learning solfége and music theory with Vincenzo Gangi.

In 2024 Arturo initiated his studies concerning jazz music taking private lessons with Professor Claudio Cusmano and is since 2025 a student of Vincenzo Bellini’s Conservatory of Catania. Parallel to his private and academic jazz studies, Arturo kept focusing on modern guitar visualisation and technique with his friend and coach Marco Di Grazia.

Bands and Projects

Throughout his career, Arturo Caflisch experimented various roles as rhythm guitarist and lead player.

After playing funk in the band Ufficio, classic rock with Asphyxia and dark ambient for Testa Cucita, the participation to pop-rock project Etnapop unlocked his passion for original music, songwriting and lead playing in 2020.

Co-founder of Metiria in 2024, Arturo Caflisch is now fully dedicated to the inspiring challenges of progressive, alternative and symphonic metal concerning odd time signatures, extended range instruments and the typical melodic and harmonic interlocking of these styles. He is at the same time particularly affectioned to the acoustic duo Songbirds he co-founded with his girlfriend and Metiria’s front-woman Aurora Amas.

Inspirations

Arturo Caflisch shapes his tone on metal legends belonging to the progressive virtuosos (especially John Petrucci of Dream Theater), the glam shredders (in particular George Lynch and Jon Levin of Dokken), the thrash kickers (like Kerry King, Jeff Hannemann and Gary Holt of Slayer), as well as the djent monsters (particularly Mårten Hagström, Fredrik Thordendal and Jens Kidman of Meshuggah) and the alternative innovators (like Nick De Pirro of Night Verses). Parallel to metal Arturo Caflisch is fascinated by the sounds of fusion (Greg Howe, Guthrie Govan, Matteo Mancuso, …) and modern jazz (Jonathan Kreisberg, Charles Altura, Julian Lage).

Style

Arturo Caflisch tries to imagine his guitar playing as a counter voice of the lead melody or, oppositely, as a percussive extension of the drums.

He tends to insert pedal notes in his arpeggiating riffs and licks as much as he loves playing artificial divisions (especially duplets and fourth note triplets) on asymmetrical times.

Other Relevant Information

Arturo gets his Bachelor in Communication and Languages with a work on Kodaly’s solfège system and is now trying to develop his own method before the end of his Master degree.

In his spare time he enjoys doing setups and modifications to his string and percussion instruments. The love for luthiery helped him developing a guitar with triple p-rail, phase control and serial, hybrid and parallel wiring.
