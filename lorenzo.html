Lorenzo Ragusa

Bass

Catania, Italy

Music Education

Born in Catania on December 17, 2000, Lorenzo Ragusa discovered his passion for music and the bass guitar at the age of fifteen. While completing his professional studies in the hospitality sector, he dedicated his personal time to musical development.

Although he received formal instruction from Cristiano Nuovo and Dino Fiorenza, he is primarily a self-taught musician, a testament to his dedication and personal research into the instrument.

Bands and Projects

From the beginning of his career, Lorenzo Ragusa actively sought collaboration with other musicians to form ensemble projects.

In 2019, he joined Symmetries, followed by MoonSpeak in 2022, with whom he released the single A Thing She Cared About. Subsequently, he focused on recording an album with the rock band Naïf.

In 2024, he co-founded the project Metiria alongside Arturo Caflisch (guitar) and Samuele Mangiaratti (drums).

Inspirations

His musical identity is shaped by a blend of modern progressive influences and classic rock foundations.

Bands: Destrage, Rush, Porcupine Tree, Opeth, Tesseract, and Mudvayne.

Bassists: Geddy Lee, Patrick Djivas, Dave LaRue, and Billy Sheehan.

Style

His approach to the bass guitar aims to balance foundational support with melodic prominence.

Whenever possible, he strives to create bass lines that serve the composition while maintaining a distinct melodic character that defines the band’s overall sound. He is proficient in advanced technical executions, including thumb-down slapping and multi-finger tapping.

Other Relevant Information

During his leisure time, he prefers to disconnect from social obligations to focus on personal recovery, often retreating to the mountains for several days to recharge his already low charged social battery.
