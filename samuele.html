Samuele Mangiaratti

Drums

Catania, Italy

Music Education

Samuele Mangiaratti (born October 21, 2001, in Catania) is an Italian drummer. Raised in Palagonia, he demonstrated a profound inclination toward music and percussion instruments since age of one when he was already improvising with household items to recreate a sort of homemade drum set.

During his formative years, frequent listening to Luciano Pavarotti — specifically the aria Nessun dorma — significantly contributed to the development of his musical sensibility. At age three, he received his first toy drum kit, consolidating a passion that evolved, three years later, into a formal course of study under the guidance of Maestro Franco Barresi.

At eleven, he complemented his percussion studies by taking three years of violin lessons with Maestra Giusy Sipala. Concurrently, he participated in the Palagonia municipal band, performing on bass drum, cymbals, and snare drum, thereby gaining extensive experience in traditional percussion.

Following a period of self-taught practice, he began advanced drum training at nineteen with Maestro Enzo Augello who prepared him for the admission to the P.I. Tchaikovsky State Conservatory in Nocera Terinese (CZ).

Within the Jazz Drumming department, he studied under Maestro Roberto Rossi earning his Bachelor at the end of the 2024/2025 academic year. He is now continuing his studies at the "Vincenzo Bellini" Conservatory in Catania.

Bands and Projects

Throughout his musical career, Samuele Mangiaratti has participated in various projects.

At fourteen, he recorded the five-track EP Deadline with the group Axes Of Hiradya, marking one of his earliest recording experiences. Subsequently, he contributed to the release of the singles Oltre il Limite, Faded, and Flames on Me in collaboration with Lie To Fine and Asso Anyone.

He is now a member of the band Metiria, where he is actively involved in songwriting and recording.

Inspirations

His primary influences include prominent bands such as Dream Theater, Avenged Sevenfold, Bullet for My Valentine, Starset, Polaris, Architects, Bring Me the Horizon, Deftones, Limp Bizkit, and Linkin Park. Among his reference drummers, he cites Mike Portnoy, Eloy Casagrande, Eric Moore, Buddy Rich, Dom Famularo, The Rev, and John Otto.

Style

His playing style is defined by a strong orientation toward creativity and innovation, with a specific focus on the interplay between rhythmic, melodic, and vocal components.

He favors a dynamic approach, adapting patterns to the flow of the composition and emphasizing the use of silence as a fundamental expressive element — a technique used to provide depth, substance, and originality to the music.

Other Relevant Information

In addition to his musical career, he cultivates interests in hiking and environmental conservation, martial arts, boxing, cycling, and culinary arts.
